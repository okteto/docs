# How Okteto Billing Works

Okteto's billing follows the same model your team already understands from tools like GitLab: **per-seat, floating, annual, with a true-up if your usage grows**. We charge for accounts in the system, not for environments or agents, and we give your team a 45-day window to clean up after one-off events like hackathons.

## The short version

- **Per seat**, like every developer tool you already buy.
- **Floating**: a seat is a slot, not a person. Reassign the slot to another email at any time at no extra cost.
- **No charge per agent or per environment.** Run as many development environments and agents per developer as your infrastructure supports.
- **45-day cleanup window.** Accounts that are less than 45 days old do not count. Run a hackathon, pilot a new team, or onboard contractors without paying. Just delete the accounts you no longer need before they age past 45 days.
- **Self-service visibility.** See your account count in the [Admin Dashboard](admin/dashboard.mdx) or via the [Okteto API](admin/okteto-api.mdx) at any time.

## What is a seat?

A seat is one user account in your Okteto instance, identified by an email address.

We do **not** count:

- The number of development environments a developer creates.
- The number of agents (Okteto, AI, or otherwise) that operate on behalf of that developer.
- Service accounts and bots that the platform itself uses.

If you bought 50 seats, you can have 50 accounts in your system. Each one can run as many environments and agents as your cluster supports.

:::note
Your contract refers to "Users" and "User Allowance". This page uses "seat" because that is how the industry talks about it. They mean the same thing.
:::

## Floating seats

A seat is not tied to a specific email forever. If `alice@yourcompany.com` leaves the team, you can reassign her seat to `bob@yourcompany.com` at no additional cost.

Floating means:

- **Reassign anytime.** Reassign a former employee's seat to their replacement, no charge.
- **No named-user lock-in.** A seat is never permanently bound to one person.
- **Contractor-friendly.** Rotating contractors in and out of seats is part of the design.

This works the same way GitLab handles seats. It is the standard model for modern developer tools.

## How the count works

This is the part most customers ask about, so here is the rule plainly:

**At any time, your billable seat count is the number of accounts in your Okteto instance that are more than 45 days old.**

That is the whole rule. Two consequences worth understanding:

1. **New accounts are free for 45 days.** Sign up 30 people for a hackathon today, they don't count. The clock is per account, not per overage event.
2. **Inactive accounts still count.** If an account is in the system, it counts (once it ages past 45 days). Deactivating, blocking, or simply abandoning it does **not** remove it from the count. The account must be **deleted**, or **reassigned** to a different email.

This is a deliberate design choice: it makes the count auditable by the customer (just count accounts in the [Admin Dashboard](admin/dashboard.mdx)) and removes ambiguity about what "active" means.

## What happens when you exceed your seats

You are not blocked when you go over. New accounts can keep being created without service interruption.

When the count of accounts older than 45 days exceeds your purchased seat allowance, here is what happens:

1. **True-up at your contracted rate.** The additional seats are billed at the per-seat price on your Order Form, pro-rated over the remaining months of your current annual term, and invoiced immediately.
2. **Renewal updated.** At the start of your next annual period, your subscription is updated to reflect the new seat count so the math is clean going forward.

If your usage is trending toward your allowance, talk to your account team early. Add-on seats purchased proactively are typically cheaper than waiting for a true-up.

## How to manage your seat count

Two tools, both free:

- **Delete accounts you no longer need.** Once an account is deleted, it disappears from the count immediately, regardless of age.
- **Reassign accounts via floating.** Change the email associated with an existing account when staff rotate. The account itself doesn't change, so it stays in your count, but you don't have to delete and recreate.

Practical pattern for one-off events: create accounts for the event, run the event, **delete** the accounts within 45 days. They never count toward your allowance.

## Checking your seat usage

**Admin Dashboard.** The [Admin Dashboard](admin/dashboard.mdx) shows your current account count, your purchased allowance, and how many accounts are within the 45-day grace window.

**API.** The same data is available programmatically via the [Okteto API](admin/okteto-api.mdx), so you can wire seat usage into your own dashboards, FinOps tools, or HR offboarding workflows.

## Frequently asked questions

### Do I get charged when a developer runs an extra environment?

No. We charge for seats, not environments. A developer with one seat can run as many environments as your cluster supports.

### What about AI agents or build agents that use Okteto on a developer's behalf?

Not counted. A seat is a human, identified by email.

### We had a hackathon. 30 outside developers signed up, used Okteto for 3 days, and never logged in again. Do we owe anything?

Only if you leave the accounts in the system past day 45. If you delete them within 45 days, they never count. Abandoned accounts continue to count once they age out of the grace window, even if no one has logged into them.

### Our team grew permanently. We have 10 extra accounts that have been in the system for 60 days. What happens?

You are now in true-up territory. We will bill the 10 seats at your contracted rate, pro-rated over the months remaining in your current term, and your renewal will reflect the new seat count.

### My team has 47 developers. We share five extra emails for contractors who rotate in and out. What do I owe?

At any moment, only 47 + 5 = 52 accounts exist in the system. Reassigning a contractor seat to a new email does not create a new account, so the count stays at 52.

### A developer left. Do I deactivate the account or delete it?

For billing purposes, **delete it** (or reassign to their replacement). Deactivation does not remove the account from the count.

### Is there a hard cap?

No. We do not block additional accounts from being created. The platform stays fully usable. The true-up settles the difference at invoicing.

## How this compares to other developer tools

For customers comparing Okteto to other tools, here is how the major models stack up.

| Mechanic | Okteto | GitLab | Atlassian (Cloud) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pricing unit | Per seat | Per seat (billable user) | Per user (monthly) or per tier (annual) |
| Floating / reassignable | Yes | Yes | Effectively no. Users are tied to accounts; you free a seat by deleting or removing the user. |
| Hard cap when over allowance | No, true-up | No, billed for overage | Yes, hard stop at plan limit |
| Counts environments / agents | No | N/A | N/A |
| What gets counted | Total accounts in the system older than 45 days | Total billable users (counted immediately) | Total users with product access (counted at invitation) |
| Grace period for new accounts | 45 days | None | None |
| Removing an account from the count | Delete or reassign | Block, deactivate, or remove | Delete or remove from synced directory |

**Where Okteto matches the industry.** Per-seat pricing, floating and reassignable seats, and a true-up at renewal are how GitLab and most modern developer tools work. We do not invent a new model.

**Where Okteto is more lenient than the industry.** The 45-day grace window for new accounts is unique. GitLab counts a user the moment they are added to the subscription. Atlassian counts at invitation, even before login. Okteto gives you 45 days to clean up, which means hackathons, pilots, and short-lived evaluations cost nothing.

**Where Okteto is similar to the industry on cleanup.** Like every other tool, you have to actively remove accounts you don't want billed. Atlassian requires deletion or directory removal; GitLab requires block, deactivate, or remove; Okteto requires deletion or reassignment. Stopping use is never enough at any of these vendors.

## Summary

The only number that matters for your Okteto bill is **how many accounts older than 45 days are in your instance**. Environments, agents, and abandoned-but-undeleted accounts all matter only if you let abandoned accounts age past 45 days. Manage your accounts the way you manage GitLab or Atlassian seats, and the math is predictable.
