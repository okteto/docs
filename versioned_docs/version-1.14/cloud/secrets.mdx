Application configuration should be passed at deployment time, not hardcoded in your code.

Okteto Secrets allows you to save application configuration in Okteto, and automatically inject it during deployment time.

## Manage Okteto Secrets from the Okteto dashboard

You can create and delete your Okteto Secrets from the `Secrets` section in the `Settings` view of the Okteto dashboard:

<p align="center">
  <Image
    src={require("@site/static/img/secrets-sidebar.png").default}
    alt="Secrets in settings"
    width="650"
  />
</p>

To create a new secret, click on the **Add Secret** button, and provide a name and a value. The value will be masked once the secret is created.

<p align="center">
  <Image
    src={require("@site/static/img/add-secret.png").default}
    alt="add a secret"
    width="450"
  />
</p>

To delete an existing secret, click on the **Delete** button on the right. You'll have to confirm your choice before the secret is deleted. Deleted secrets can't be recovered, so be careful when doing this.

<p align="center">
  <Image
    src={require("@site/static/img/delete-secret.png").default}
    alt="delete a secret"
    width="650"
  />
</p>

## Accessing Okteto Secrets from your Okteto Pipeline

Okteto Secrets are automatically injected in your [Okteto CLI](cloud/okteto-cli.mdx) commands as environment variables.

Follow [this tutorial](https://github.com/okteto/pipeline-with-generated-secrets) for a sample using Okteto Secrets in an Okteto Pipeline.
